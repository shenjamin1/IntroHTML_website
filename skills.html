I can teach you the fundamentals of how to read notes on sheet music!

Here's what you have to know to read notes on sheet music:

  • Which clef it's on.
  • What key each note corresponds to on the staff.
  • The note value of a note.
  • The key signature with sharps or flats.

There is a treble clef which is on the top staff and a bass clef that is on the bottom staff.

Each note corresponds to a key. The correspondings are different on each clef. Here are some charts to help.



You can refer to these acronyms to help you memorize.


Every note has its own note value.

There are whole notes, half notes, quarter notes, eighth notes, and sixteenth notes. Here is a visual chart.

Whole notes get 4 beats. Half notes get 2 beats. Quarter notes get 1 beat. Eight notes get 1/2 a beat. Sixteenth notes get 1/4 a beat.

These rules are important for when you read time signatures and tempos. For now, we are learning how to read notes.


Sheet music can also have key signatures that will determine if there is a sharp or flat at each corresponding note.

Here is a graph to help you recognize these key signatures.

For example, in a G major piece, you would play F sharp instead of normal F.
